# Jade Reliquary

This icon language treats sacred architecture as a layered reliquary rather than a flat badge. The structure must read first through silhouette: a tiered jade roof, a luminous ivory plinth, and a ceremonial axis that descends into steps. Every mass should feel meticulously crafted, with deliberate hierarchy from the roof crown to the entry stair, so the asset still reads clearly at game size while carrying the authority of a main hall.

Color should carry social rank. Roofs hold the cold authority of weathered jade and blue-black lacquer; walls and terraces glow with warm parchment stone; timber and column details sit between them as the handcrafted bridge. Gold appears only where ritual importance demands it: ridge lines, finials, and selective trim. The result should look painstakingly calibrated, never loud, and always like the work of someone with deep expertise in stylized architectural painting.

The 2.5D viewpoint must remain uncompromised. The building is seen from a diagonal overhead angle, with visible roof planes, compressed verticals, and a broad ceremonial platform that grounds the composition. Secondary side halls should support the central mass, not compete with it. Depth is communicated through stacked planes, controlled shadows, and precise edge rhythm rather than through photographic realism.

Detail must feel labored over with master-level care, but not noisy. Doors, steps, columns, railings, and ridge ornaments are included only where they strengthen recognition and craftsmanship. The final asset should feel game-ready: instantly legible as the sect's principal hall, visually rich on close inspection, and refined enough to sit beside finished production UI without exposing itself as placeholder art.
